Preparations

The first day you bring your Elkhound home can be quite hectic if you're unprepared. If you plan in advance, the transistion will be easier on both of you.

Preparation Checklist
Item Notes
☐ Food When buying dog food, first start out with the brand that the breeder or prior owner was feeding your Elkhound and slowly mix in the food of your choice. If you're unsure of what dog food to get, I would recommend going to Dog Food Advisor.
☐ Treats Soft and small treats work best for training purposes.
☐ Food and Water Bowls Preferably stainless steel or ceramic.
☐ Storage bin You will want a large storage bin to store the dog's food.
☐ Toys Get a variety of toys such as a kong toy, stuffed toy with a squeaker, balls, rope toys, and nylabones. You can get cow ears and natural beef bones for chewing as well. Do not get ham bones or rawhides as they can be dangerous.
☐ Collar and Leash Start off with a small collar if you get a puppy. An adult Elkhound will typically need a large sized collar. A short lease is preferable because you don't want your dog to take lead while walking.
☐ Grooming supplies Get a medium tooth comb or rake. Also pick up some dog shampoo and pet ear wash solution.
☐ Dog Nail Clippers If you plan on clipping your Elkhound's nails, make sure you have a set of nail clippers on hand.
☐ Pee pads and cleaners While potty training a puppy, you should have pee pads on hand. Also get stain and odor-removing cleaners for when your puppy does have accidents.
☐ Dog Bed If you get a wire crate, make sure you get a fleece pet bed for inside. Otherwise, get a nice pet bed of your choice for your Elkhound to sleep on.
☐ Travel Crate Even if you plan to let your Elkhound roam around in the back of the car, you should have a travel crate while it's a puppy.
☐ Wire Crate You should get a wire crate if you are interested in crate training.
☐ Find a vet Use GreatVets to find a vet near you.
